Chess

The Struggle Against Error

As far back as I can remember, I've always played chess. It is truly a game of discipline and focus, which was an antithesis of my nature. As a child, most of my opponents were adults. Dragged to outdoor tables on Yonge, to tournaments, to my Dad's friends homes, I played, a child amongst men. It represents that constant struggle of anticipation, calculation, and patience.

My Library

My Love of Books

I have always loved books. As a child, the library or bookstore was my escape to new worlds or a deeper understanding of this one. History seen thru Dickens & Dumas, brave new worlds of Herbert, Asimov, and Tolkien, and social insights of Gladwell... I could not get enough. But now I walk into bookstores with regret, realizing there is not time enough in the day and in a lifetime to breathe it all in!

What a Wonderful World

My Love of Travel

“Travel makes one modest. You see what a tiny place you occupy in the world.” – Gustave Flaubert
From laying fearfully on a tiger in Thailand, to stalking a trio of rhinocerous in South Africa, these are the memories that make life worth living. The language, architecture, and food of different cultures, reminds you that though we are different, we are very much the same. Step out, see the world, and truly embrace life!.

With These Hands

Dabbling in Woodwork

As a child many considered me to be more intellectually inclined. But as I got older, with the intent of saving money, I began learning and enjoying doing small renovations on my house. From building the multi-level deck, electrical work, wainscotting and woodwork, I have treated each of my projects as both an intellectual and physical exercise. Goju Ryu Karate

The Internal and External Struggle

Inspired by martial arts films, I walked into a dojo as a teenager expecting to become a lethal weapon... only to fall in love with tradition and philosophy of Goju Ryu Karate. You realize that the true adversary is yourself, physically and mentally. Can you develop the preciseness required in kata, or tactical adaptability required in kumite. How can one overcome an opponent if they are a slave to their own limitations?
