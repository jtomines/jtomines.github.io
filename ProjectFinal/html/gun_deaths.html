Grading of State Gun Control Laws vs Firearm Mortality Rate

Each year since 2014, the Gifford Law Center, an advocate for proper gun control, releases a score card evaluating the strength of the gun control laws for each state. The letter grade assigned to each state was converted to a percentage, where an A+ received 98%, and F received 50%. The shading of each state on the map of the US represents those grades. Darker shades represent stronger gun laws for the selected year. NOTE: For years prior to 2014 selected using the slider, the map will display the gun law grades of year 2014. If the slider is used to select year 2018, the map will display the gun law grades of year 2017.

The Center for Disease Control has recorded both the number of deaths and the mortality rate per 100,000 people attributed to firearms. The red spots, situated in the center of each state, represents that mortality rate recorded for that year. The larger the red spot, the higher the mortality rate attributed to firearms.

When comparing both sets of data on the map, it is apparent that the mortality rate drops signficantly for states with stronger gun laws. This is contrary to the rhetoric that gun advocates claim.
